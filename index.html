I build data-driven tools that help agencies and public health teams turn complex environmental data into clear, actionable decisions. My work spans prioritizing abandoned mine cleanup across the American Southwest to mapping pathogen hotspots in low-resource settings worldwide.

My work sits at the intersection of environmental engineering, spatial modeling, and public health. I combine field data, machine learning, and interactive visualization to make findings accessible to the decision-makers who need them most.

What I Work On

Abandoned Mine Hazard Prioritization
I developed an interactive R Shiny tool that integrates water quality data, GIS layers, and user-adjustable vulnerability weights to help agencies identify and rank abandoned mine sites for remediation across Arizona and the Southwest.

Wastewater-Based Epidemiology and Decision Frameworks
As part of the NSF Pandemic ESCAPE center, I develop decision algorithms and partner maps for wastewater surveillance programs, helping public health agencies move from raw detection data to coordinated action. Sites span Arizona, Kentucky, Alaska, and Uganda.

Spatial Modeling for Pathogen Hotspot Detection
I use hydraulic/hydrologic modeling, machine learning, and GIS to identify priority sampling locations in areas with limited infrastructure. This work is currently focused on non-sewered communities in Uganda.
