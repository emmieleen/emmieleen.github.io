--- layout: home title: Emma Bonham subtitle: Postdoctoral researcher using GIS, spatial modeling, and environmental data to support public health and hazard decision-making cover-img: /assets/img/mine-vulnerability-tool.png --- I build data-driven tools that help agencies and public health teams turn complex environmental data into clear, actionable decisions — from prioritizing abandoned mine cleanup across the American Southwest to mapping pathogen hotspots in low-resource settings worldwide. My work sits at the intersection of environmental engineering, spatial modeling, and public health. I combine field data, machine learning, and interactive visualization to make findings accessible to the decision-makers who need them most. --- ## What I Work On **Abandoned Mine Hazard Prioritization** I developed an interactive R Shiny tool that integrates water quality data, GIS layers, and user-adjustable vulnerability weights to help agencies identify and rank abandoned mine sites for remediation across Arizona and the Southwest. **Wastewater-Based Epidemiology & Decision Frameworks** As part of the NSF Pandemic ESCAPE center, I develop decision algorithms and partner maps for wastewater surveillance programs — helping public health agencies move from raw detection data to coordinated action. Sites span Arizona, Kentucky, Alaska, and Uganda. **Spatial Modeling for Pathogen Hotspot Detection** I use hydraulic/hydrologic modeling, machine learning, and GIS to identify priority sampling locations in areas with limited infrastructure — work currently focused on non-sewered communities in Uganda. --- ## Recent Work - 🗺️ [Projects](/projects) — Tools, models, and publications - 👩‍🔬 [About Me](/aboutme) — Background, skills, and education - 📬 [Contact](/contact) — Get in touch --- *Currently based at Arizona State University · Hamilton Lab Group · Biodesign Center for Environmental Health Engineering*
